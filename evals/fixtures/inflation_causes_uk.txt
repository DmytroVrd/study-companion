Topic: Inflation and monetary reforms

Inflation is the depreciation of money caused by excessive growth of the money supply in
circulation, which appears through rising prices.

Main causes of inflation can be divided into external and internal causes.

External causes:
- global structural crises: raw material crisis, energy crisis, currency crisis;
- illegal export of gold and foreign currency;
- reduction of income from foreign trade;
- negative balance of foreign trade and balance of payments.

Internal non-monetary causes:
- violation of economic proportions;
- cyclical development of the economy;
- monopolization of production;
- unbalanced investment;
- state-monopolistic pricing;
- credit expansion;
- instability of the social and economic situation in the state.

Internal monetary causes:
- budget deficit;
- growth of public debt;
- excessive money emission;
- increase in credit instruments due to expansion of the credit system;
- increase in the speed of money circulation.
